Taking the Test

Most colleges will require either an SAT or ACT test score and sometimes SAT subject test scores. Here is a brief overview of each test.

SAT

The SAT is a 3 hour (3 hour and 50 minute with optional essay) test that has two sections: Writing and Language

  • Chinese with Listening
  • Italian
  • German
  • German with Listening
  • Modern Hebrew
  • Latin
  • Japanese with Listening

Often, a college will require 2-3 subject tests. Some schools require specific ones; others let you choose which ones to take. People tend to take the test in the subject they just learned in school (i.e. taking the Biology subject test after taking AP Bio). Whatever you choose to take, just practice as much as you can. CollegeBoard conveniently provides descriptions and practice questions for all subject tests. Plus, there are tons of resources like Barron's and Princeton Review that will give you not only content review but also practice questions and tests.

Sending Your Scores

Some colleges will require you to send your test scores. More and more schools are becoming test-optional. Some schools will allow score-choice, which means you can send whichever scores you want. Others Others will require all of your scores. Many schools also superscore which means that they will take the highest score across multiple sittings. For schools that require score reports, here's a step by step process of how to send your scores:

CollegeBoard

  1. Go to https://www.collegeboard.org/.
  2. Sign in
  3. Click on "My SAT"

ACT

  1. Go to http://www.act.org/
  2. Sign in to "ACT Test"
  3. Sign into your account
  4. Click on "Send Your Scores"
  5. Click on the test score(s) you want to submit

  6. TAKE CAUTION: when sending scores, make sure you send them well in advance. It often takes about 1-3 weeks for them to be sent by CollegeBoard/ACT and processed by the school(s) you're sending them to. If you are taking any of these tests, the latest testing date suggested for early admission is October and the latest testing date suggested for regular admission is November.
