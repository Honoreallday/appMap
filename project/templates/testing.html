Taking the Test

 Most colleges will require an SAT or ACT test score and sometimes SAT subject test scores. It is highly recommended that you take your first test during the fall of your junior year. Between the SAT and ACT, it is best to try each test to see which one is best for you. Here is a brief overview of each test.

SAT

  • Time: 3 hours (+ 50 minutes w/ optional essay)
  • Sections: 3 (Reading, Writing and Language, Math)
  • Scores: 400-1600 (essay: 3 domains scored 2-8 each)

ACT

  • Time: 2 hours 55 minutes (+ 40 minutes w/ optional essay)
  • Sections: 4 (Reading, English, Math, Science)
  • Scores: 1-36 (essay: domain scores 2-12 each)

SAT Subject Tests

You may have heard something about SAT IIs or SAT subject tests. Currently, the CollegeBoard offers 20 subject tests:

  • Math Level 1
  • Math Level 2
  • Biology E/M(Ecological/Molecular)

  • Physics
  • English
  • U.S. History
  • World History
  • Spanish
  • Spanish with Listening
  • French
  • French with Listening
  • Chinese with Listening
  • Italian
  • German
  • German with Listening
  • Modern Hebrew
  • Latin
  • Japanese with Listening
  • Korean with Listening

 Often, a college will require 2-3 subject tests. Some schools require specific ones; others let you choose which ones to take. People tend to take the test in the subject they just learned in school (i.e. taking the Biology subject test after taking AP Bio).

  Whatever you choose to take,practice as much as you can. CollegeBoard and ACT conveniently provides descriptions and practice questions. Plus, there are tons of resources like Barron's and Princeton Review that will give you not only content review but also practice questions and tests.

Sending Your Scores

 Some colleges will require you to send your test scores. More and more schools are becoming test-optional, and some schools will allow score-choice, which means you can send whichever scores you want. Others will require all of your scores. Many schools also superscore which means that they will take the highest score across multiple sittings. For schools that require score reports, here's a step by step process of how to send your scores:

CollegeBoard

  1. Go to https://www.collegeboard.org/ and sign in.



  2. Click on "My SAT".



  3. Click on "Send Available Scores Now" and then click "Send Additional Score Reports".



  4. Search your school's name, click on it, and then click add to add each school one by one. Then, hit continue once you're finished adding all of your schools.



  5. If your school offers score-choice, then you have the option to select the test scores you want to send. You can change which scores will be sent by clicking on "Choose Scores".



  6. Once, you're satisfied with your score report, then you can click continue to go to the payment section where you will put in your credit card information. From there, when you hit submit, you should be redirected to a confirmation page with your order information.

ACT

  1. Go to http://www.act.org/.



  2. Sign in to "ACT Test."



  3. Sign into your account.



  4. Click on "Send Your Scores."



  5. Click on the test score(s) you want to submit, and click continue



  6. Enter in the information of the school(s) you are sending the scores to.



  7. Once you're done adding your schools, click "continue."



  8. You should be taken to the payment section where you will put in your credit card information. Once you do that and hit "submit," you should be taken to a confirmation page with your order information.

    9.   TAKE CAUTION: when sending scores, make sure you send them well in advance. It often takes about 1-3 weeks for them to be sent by CollegeBoard/ACT and processed by the school(s) you're sending them to. If you are taking any of these tests, the latest testing date suggested for early admission is October and the latest testing date suggested for regular admission is November. If you plan to take the tests on these dates, CollegeBoard and ACT offer students the option to send 4 score reports for free. Otherwise, you will have to pay $12 for each score report. If paying for score reports becomes a financial burden for you/your family, you can request fee waivers for them. It is best to talk to your guidance/college counselor about obtaining fee waivers.
