Pre-Senior Year

  • Programs and College Prep

    • No matter where you are in highschool, its never too early to start preping for college, and programs can be the best way to do that.
    • Whether they be summer, in school, or after school, the more the merrier. So go on and start your search at our "Programs" page.

    Freshman-Junior year

August

  • The College Search!!

    Its time to figure out where you want to apply! And even though this may be one of the easier steps, It is easily on of the most important. So make sure to take it seriously, and be true to yourself

    • Make sure you have a good proportion of safeties to schools that you think will be a reach
    • Don't be scared to reach out to admissions and ask what they're school has to offer and what they want in students (it may give you a leg up)
    • Try out the College board's searching system as a starting point, and branch out from there: Link.

    Junior-Senior summer

  • Finalizing Test scores

    Test Scores are an important part of the application process, and everyone wants the best score possible. But testing is expensive, time consuming, and has limited space. So the sooner you can get a score your happy with, the better.

    • If you feel you can do better and want to retest, then just make sure there is ample time to have the tests scored and sent.
    • ACT test dates and more: Link.
    • SAT test dates and more: Link.

    August-ish

September

  • Early action/desicion

    Early action and decision can be a huge deal for some of you, and for others it won't be. EA and ED are when schools will allow you to apply to an earlier deadline and recieve an early decision. Just no that ED is binding in that if your accepted you are required to attend (a good way to apply to your dream school).

    • For those applying ED, you'll be on an expidited track trying to fill out your application because the deadline is usually november first. So in your case, it's likely best if you start that application (you are only allowed to do one ED app, since if your accepted, you'll be enrolled) as soon as it becomes available
    • For anyone doing EA, you will also be on an expidited track. EA and ED are completley the same in most respects, the big difference being that it's non binding.
    • Deciding to apply some where ED or EA is an big decision to make, but just know that you do not have to if you don't want to. There are defenitly benefits to doing so (earlier notification, higher acceptance rate) you don;t lose very much if it's something that youd ont decide to persue.

    September

  • Recommendations

    Recommendations can make the difference in a lot of your application, so it's very important ot pick the right teachers, and to give them lots of time to do it right.

    • If you already know who you'll be asking, try and reach out to them in the beggining of the year and get it over with quick, because once the school year gets going and there getting bombarded by requests from other students, they might not have the "creative juice" left to depict you in all of your awesomeness.
    • And this one may be a no-brainer, but pick a the teachers that will make you look the best, that may not be the one your closest with (or the one you like the most). Just try and pick the person that understands you best as a student and a learner. Because those often make for the best references.

    As soon as school starts (August-September)

  • Scholarships

    Start looking for scholarships, becasuse they will come and go with time. And some of the big ones come by real early.

    • Just remember that this comes second to actually applying for school (especially if your doing EA or ED). There are thousands of scholarships and if you have to miss one to finnish one of your apps in time, so be it, there will be others.

    September

  • Starting The Application

    Now it's tome to actually start the dirty work. You've come a long way, and you've got a ways to go, but progress is progress. SO LET'S BEGIN. First steps first, make an account with the application website (not all schools will use the two listed below).

    late September

October

  • Essay Work

    They take longer than most would think, get going early and put some work into it.

    • If your doing EA or ED your really on the clock, so make sure you pace yourself.

    mid october

  • School specific essays

    Along with the regular old common app essay comes school specific essays. And these can be weird and much more challenging than the others

    • DO YOUR RESEARCH, an hour surfing through an institutions web page can tell you a lot about what a school wants in a student, and with that you'll know what to pitch.
    • It may help to research past essays, but remember to make yours your own. As cliche as it may be, the best essay is often the one from the heart.

    October

November

  • SUBMISSION (EA/ED)

    Time to turn those Early Decision/Action apps in!!

    • For the vast majority of EA/ED applications, the due date is november 1st. which is mighty soon, but luckily it's only 1 applicaiton so it's not that hard of a task to accomplish. Many have done it before you, and you will do it too.

    November 1st (at the latest)

  • Every other essay

    Now that EA/ED are out of the way, it's time to get going on everything else. The regular decision due date is usually between jan.1st and 15th, so the clock is ticking.

    • For the vast majority of EA/ED applications, the due date is november 1st. which is mighty soon, but luckily it's only one applicaiton so it's not that hard of a task to accomplish. Many have done it before you, and you will do it too.
    • With the amount of essays you'll likely be dealing with now, you gotta be smart about how you do it. To save yourself the pain, never rewrite an essay. If you can use the same essay (or peices of one), do it. You may feel weird about it at first, but when your coming around winter break abd you cant even think straight anymore you'll thank us.

    November - December

    • December

    • Finalizing applications and essays

      Now it may be only be december but application deadlines are closer than you think, and nobody wants to have to spend there whole winter break writing essays, so be dilligent and get them done.

      • All the EA/ED apps should have been turned in, so your only focus should be wrapping up everything you have left. From essay touch-ups, to re-reading all of your "short answer" answers. You want to make this thing spotless before submission
      • Since most apps will be do around new years (jan 1st - 10th) you should aim to have the bulk of them mostly done by, or before winter break. That way if you find it in you too look them over on christmas or something, you can, but if you don't you can leave them be knowing that they're completed
      • There are still applications that will have deadlines in febuary (and some in march!!) so if you have the extra time, don't be afraid to use. Just know yourself and whether or not you'll remember to get to it.

      Early-mid December

    • EA/ED DECISIONS!

      Now is the time to start checking your mailbox and your emails, because EA/ED decisoins are rolling in.

      • Remember that this decision will not make or break your college experience (coming from someone who got rejected from their "dream school" on ED). There are still plenty of schools to apply to and here back from . And if you didn't get in where you wanted then you'll still get in somewhere great
      • If you are accepted then your done, given that ED is binding (and we're assuming you'll want to attend if it's EA) you don't have much else to do. Just be careful, because there have been cases of students getting in trouble with their ED school for not withdrawing all of their other applications after getting accepted.

      December 15th

      • Submit your other Apps.

        Your on the home stretch here. If you've been accepted EA or ED, than you shouldn't do this. Otherwise just give those applications one last read through and hit submit. It'll feel like a weight off you shoulders.

        • If you get "Defered" on EA or ED it is essentially like a waitlist, where they'll throw your application into the regular decision applicant pool, and review it again. So if this is you, than go ahead and submit all the other applications you have, because once you are deffered it becomes non-binding.
        • Take a break, relax, kick back. There's still work to be done but this is a huge milestone and it's time that you started getting sleep again. enjoy christmas and new years (if they havent passed yet), and get ready to hit the ground running when the time comes.

        dec 20-jan 10th

      January

      • Application Trackers

        Now that you've submitted all of your apps you should be getting emails from all the schools prompting you to make an account for something like an "application tracker". Don't worry, your not behind, this is just the uglier part of the process.

        • Just to help yourself out, I'd suggest that you use the same password and email for each, i swear you'll thank me in the long run. (just make sure not to tell ANYONE.)
        • These accounts you're making are what will help you find out what you still need to turn in (i.e. reccomendations they're waiting for, test scores, mid-year/final transcripts) and eventually where you'll find your decisions. So get in habit of checking it frequently. And if you don't understand something your seeing dont be scared to make a call or send an email, the admissions office WANTS TO HELP.

        mid january (or a bit after you submit an app)

      • Financial Aid Applications

        This is arguably one of the most annoying, and cumbersome parts of the process, I'm warning you now, but it's a necesary one. Its a lot to explain though, so rather than waist my breath (that was a joke, get it, because this is a computer, it doesn't actually breathe, y'all don't seem as entertained about this as I am) I'm just going to link our page that ALREADY EXPLAINS IT ALL.

        late january

      March

      • Submit Financial Aid Applications

        Time to turn those bad boys in. It wasn't fun, I'm sure, but as long as you took your time and did them right, they should be good.

        • Given that this is money we're talking about, please be careful. When I got to the end of my financial aid applications I was pretty ready to throw my computer at the wall, but i'm sure that wouldn't have helped anything. Just take a second to breathe, run through it all on more time to make sure EVERYTING is right (the slightest mistake may have you doing it all over again, like I had to) and then submit.

        mid March

      • SCHOLARSHIPS

        If your still alive then you're doing well, but there's still more. In terms of the college applications you've just gotta wait to here back (usually some time in may). But with this free time it's probably a good idea to really throw yourself at some scholarships. They're not necesary, but they will DEFINETLY help, and some of them actually enroll you in programs to give you support during college so definetly keep you eyes open.

        • I know it may be hard to really get back in the chair and do more apps, but it's well worth it. And you have all those perfected college application essays that I'm sure can be used for more than a few of your scholarship apps.
        • But do make sure to give yourself a break, it's been a long time coming and you've accomplished quite a bit already. Go see your friends, dust off that PS4 you haven't touched in a while, TREAT YO-SELF.

      • THE BIG DAY

        It's that time, you've been waiting so long for this, the emails should be rolling in right about now. Just relax, take a breath, do some yoga, and see what they've said.

        • Side note, the actual decisions will not be in the emails themselves (95% of the time). Usually the email is a link that redirects you to the application tracker where you'll have to log in, and then you can click to see their decision.
        • Also, as a some words of encourgemant, it is the person that makes the college, not the other way around. So if your not going to your number 1, 2, or even 10 school, just know that it's up to you to make the best out of what your given. And If you really hate it that much, you can always transfer. 1/3 of all students do it at some point, so you'll be in good company.

        late March

      April

      • Making the decision

        What ever your options are, the vast majority of you have some thinking to do. So here are some tips to make the big decision.

        • Just remember, your best option may not be your #1 school. Just think, the financial aid offers should be out so who gave you the most money, what does job recruitment look like post graduation, do they have all the support for students that you feel you'd need. Back when you were picking these schools it was easy to go off looks, or feels, but try to be more practical now, and if your mind hasn't changed, all the better.
        • Also, if you don't like the aid they offered you, reach out to the school. If they accepted you, that means they want you on campus. So just try shooting an email out, be respectful, and tell them that money may be the deciding factor. You'd be surprised how willing they might be to fight for you.
        • Try to visit if you can, this seems obvious but if you can actually see the school it may just help you finalize your decision. And if you can't afford to travel, reach out to the school, they may have some programs in place or (if your nice enough) may be willing to fly you out. Remember, they wouldn't have accepteted you if they didn't want you on campus.
        • And lastly, talk to some students, Join the "Admitted Students" facebook page if there is one. Try and here what it's actually like to live and learn there from someone who isn't trying to "sell" you the schoolm because those opinions will be the most honest.

        Mid April

      May

      • ENROLL

        The time has come, May 1st, national enrollment day. Almost all schools will require you to finalize your decision by this date so be ready.

        • Whatever you've decided, college is college, and you'll have one hell of a time.
        • And go ahead and buy some of that college merch, flex your school a lil bit, get that pride up, because your going to college.
        • If there's an enrollment fee and you can't afford it, just reach out to the school. Most will waive it for students that meet certain financial criteria.
        • And there are usually some forms that go along with enrolling at your school. So once you've made your decision, gather the family, make a whole thing out of it, and celebrate your succesess.

        May 1st

      June

      • Wrap it up

        Your just about done, all that's left is to graduate and enjoy your summer (some of you may have already done that), and let your school tell you what's left to do.

        • Now every school is different, so unfortunetly we can't help you much past here. But don't worry, Where ever your going, they'll keep in good contact with you and tell you whenever they need something. Just make sure to check your email often (personal, and the one the school makes for you).
        • Also remember to be proud of yourself. It's been a LONG RIDE but you've made it, pat yourself on the damn back and relax.
        • And don't forget about scholarships. Yes, there are still some left, and every penny helps, so never stop.

        June-august
